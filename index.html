Welcome to my data science portfolio!

by Joshua

Who am I?

As a data scientist, I specialize in extracting valuable insights from complex data sets using my analytical, statistical, programming, and problem-solving skills. My expertise in data analysis, machine learning, and data visualization enables me to help organizations make data-driven decisions and uncover hidden patterns, trends, and opportunities.

What I created?

In my data science portfolio, you will find a diverse collection of projects that showcase my proficiency in various data science techniques and domains. These projects are carefully selected to represent my ability to tackle real-world challenges, analyze complex data sets, and deliver valuable insights.

Each project includes a detailed description of the problem statement, objectives, methodology, tools and technologies used, and the results achieved. Many of the projects also provide links to code repositories and live demos, allowing you to explore my work in greater depth.

What I have?

As an experienced data scientist, my expertise spans machine learning, deep learning, statistical modeling, data visualization, natural language processing, and more. I am proficient in Python, SQL, and various machine learning libraries and frameworks, as well as big data and cloud computing technologies.

My projects demonstrate skills in data manipulation, cleaning, preprocessing, and feature engineering, along with implementing diverse machine learning algorithms and models. I have a strong track record of collaborating with cross-functional teams, effective communication, and managing end-to-end data science projects.

Featured Projects

Here is a list of featured projects:

English-Tagalog-Translator

My English to Tagalog Translator is a language translation tool that utilizes natural language processing techniques to offer translations between English and Tagalog. While the accuracy may be limited due to fine-tuning on 7,000 training data points, it still provides a useful starting point for understanding the language context. The translator tool, running on a CPU-only server, may not be the fastest, but it demonstrates my initial efforts in natural language processing and serves as a foundation for future improvements in both performance and accuracy.
